Everything you need to know about AutoReview

📝 About Reviews
How do I submit a car review on AutoReview? ▼
Click on the "Write Review" button in the navigation bar or visit submit.html. Fill in your car details, rate different aspects like performance, comfort, mileage, safety, and write your detailed experience. Once submitted, your review will appear on the Reviews page immediately.
Are the reviews on AutoReview genuine? ▼
Yes. We have a strict review moderation policy. All user-submitted reviews are checked for spam, fake content, and promotional language before being published. We do not accept paid reviews or manufacturer-sponsored content. Our expert reviews are based on real-world test drives of 1000+ km.
Can I edit or delete my review after submitting? ▼
Currently, reviews cannot be edited directly from the website. If you wish to update or delete your review, please contact us at reviews@autoreview.in with your name, car model, and the changes you'd like to make. We will process your request within 48 hours.
How is the overall rating calculated? ▼
The overall rating shown on car cards is a weighted average of all user-submitted ratings and our expert review score. Parameters include: Performance (20%), Comfort (20%), Fuel Efficiency (15%), Features (15%), Safety (15%), Service (10%), and Value for Money (5%).
What information should I include in my review? ▼
A good review includes: your car variant and year, how long you've owned it, real-world mileage, performance experience, comfort on city and highway roads, features you use daily, after-sales service experience, and whether you'd recommend it. The more detail you provide, the more helpful it is for other buyers.

Should I buy a petrol or diesel car in 2024? ▼
It depends on your usage. Choose Diesel if you drive 2000+ km/month, mostly on highways, and plan to keep the car for 5+ years. Choose Petrol if you drive less than 1500 km/month, mostly in the city, and want lower maintenance costs. Consider CNG or Hybrid for maximum fuel savings in city driving. Electric is ideal if you have home charging and drive under 100 km/day.
What is ADAS and do I need it? ▼
ADAS stands for Advanced Driver Assistance Systems. It includes features like Automatic Emergency Braking, Lane Keep Assist, Adaptive Cruise Control, Blind Spot Monitoring, and Forward Collision Warning. It significantly improves safety, especially on highways. If your budget allows, we recommend choosing a car with at least Level 1 ADAS. Cars like Hyundai Creta, MG Astor, and Tata Nexon offer ADAS in India.
How do I check a used car before buying? ▼
Before buying a used car: (1) Check the RC and insurance documents, (2) Verify chassis and engine number, (3) Get a full service history from the dealer, (4) Check for accident history on Vahan portal, (5) Test drive on city roads and highway, (6) Inspect for rust, paint mismatch, and tyre wear, (7) Get a pre-purchase inspection from a trusted mechanic, (8) Check the odometer reading against service records.
